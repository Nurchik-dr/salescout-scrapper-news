Furor Over Chinese Spy Balloon Leads to a Diplomatic Crisis

By Victoria Yu • 3 min read

The Pentagon called the object, which has flown from Montana to Kansas, an “intelligence gathering” balloon. Beijing said it was a weather balloon blown off course.

WASHINGTON — Secretary of State Antony J. Blinken on Friday canceled a weekend trip to Beijing after a Chinese spy balloon was sighted above the Rocky Mountain state of Montana, igniting a frenzy of media coverage and political commentary over a new chapter in the turbulent relationship between Washington and Beijing.

Chinese officials insisted it was a civilian craft and accused Washington of overreacting. U.S. officials, however, argued the craft had crossed sensitive military zones, sharpening an already tense debate in Congress and among allies.
